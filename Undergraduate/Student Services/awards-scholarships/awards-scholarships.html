Finances, Awards & Scholarships

Post-secondary education is an investment that will pay dividends throughout your life. But it's not always easy to afford. UBC offers resources, information and sources of funding for students, as well as financial planning support to get you where you want to go. LFS students can connect with their Enrolment Services professional (ESP) for guidance. Learn more here.

Awards and scholarships for LFS students

UBC and LFS are proud to recognize and celebrate your achievements through a number of awards, scholarships and bursaries. Awards and scholarships are a great way to finance your education, so be sure to check them out. You can find a list of the different kinds of awards and scholarships offered here.

How to know if you are eligible

It's important to know whether you're eligible for a given scholarship or bursary. (For example, you will need to carry a minimum course load to be eligible.) This page in the UBC Academic Calendar walks you through the guidelines governing awards and scholarships at both the undergraduate and graduate level.

Applying for consideration

Many awards require students to not only be strong academically, but to also present other kinds of skills, passions or involvement. We can't keep track of every student, so we want to hear from you if you think you'd be a strong candidate for an LFS award! If you'd like to be considered for a recommended award, we encourage you to submit an application here. Please indicate on the form which award(s) you would like to be considered for.

Please note, you must meet the eligibility criteria listed in the UBC Academic Calendar. Only those nominated for an award will be notified.

Still have questions?

Depending on the nature of your inquiry, you may need to contact LFS Student Services or your Enrolment Services professional (ESP).

Contact LFS Student Services if your questions relate to:

      LFS-specific awards; or
      thanking a donor.

Contact your ESP if your questions relate to:

      student loans;
      being in financial distress; or
      financial planning, tuition and fees.
