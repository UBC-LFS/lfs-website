Student Services

The LFS Student Services team provides academic advising and support for all undergraduate students in a safe, welcoming and respectful environment. Through drop-in and booked advising appointments, we help you navigate your academic and non-academic journey at UBC and in LFS, answering your questions and connecting you with resources and support services across campus. From orientation to graduation, we work together with campus partners, your LFS|US and other LFS student groups to put on events and programming to support your academic success and help you to have some fun while you're here! When things aren't going well, you want to celebrate your success, grab a snack or work on the Student Services puzzle, we encourage you to come and see us.

Medical & Emotional Difficulties

It is possible that during your time in LFS you may encounter personal challenges or unforeseen circumstances that have a negative impact on your academic attendance or performance. We are here to help, and what you share is held in confidence. We strongly encourage you to connect with us as soon as something arises so we can be best positioned to assist you in connecting with resources, as well as navigating how to minimize the negative impact on your academics.

Faculty members are in a unique position in that you see students on a regular basis and can often observe changes in behaviour, attendance or academics that indicate a student may be experiencing challenges outside the classroom (illness, personal challenges, family tragedies, etc.). If you have concerns about a student's wellbeing, please take the time to educate yourself about Assisting Students in Distress, and notify LFS Student Services for any cases that you feel our support would be beneficial. We can often liaise with other resources and support services on campus to ensure the student is receiving the support they need, as well as advise the student on how to manage their whole academic portfolio in the face of challenge. We are available to provide guidance and support for you as well as your students, and encourage you to reach out to us with any questions or concerns.

We strongly encourage all faculty to retain a copy of the 'Green Folder,' the mental health and wellbeing resource guide for Assisting Students in Distress provided by our colleagues in UBC Student Development and Services. If you would like a hard copy, please contact LFS Student Services to request one.

[/accordion] [accordion title="Grade Submission Deadlines"]

As per the FSC (Faculty Service Centre) User Guide, final grades are due 7 business days from the exam date OR 5 business days from the exam date, if the exam is written on the last two days of the exam period. For Directed Studies, Undergraduate Thesis and Essay courses, we kindly ask that instructors use the end of term dates for grade submission deadlines. We appreciate your commitment to meeting these deadlines as late submissions can prevent students from meeting eligibility requirements for year promotion, academic standing evaluations, graduation or other critical stages of their academic progression. If you have concerns about meeting these deadlines, we kindly ask you notify LFS Student Services so that we may work together to ensure students records are up to date and impacts to their progress are mitigated.

[/accordion] [accordion title="Registration, Exam & Scheduling Inquiries"]

We work together with the Associate Dean, Academic and Program Directors to ensure the Summer and Winter schedules are created, published, and maintained, and also collect and submit requests for final exams. Should you have any questions about registration, exams or scheduling, please direct your inquiries as follows:

  • Course & Student Record Management: for questions regarding registration of students in your courses (exceptions to pre-requisites, management of wait lists, etc.), submitting change of grade requests (via email or the 'Change of Grade' form via the FSC), please email students@landfood.ubc.ca

  • Course & Exam Administration: for questions about the scheduling details of your courses (terms, days, times, locations, instructor assignments, seat restrictions, etc.), formal exam schedules, please email lfs.scheduling@ubc.ca

[/accordion] [accordion title="Booking a Classroom"]

To view room availability and book a classroom outside of your scheduled teaching commitments, please refer to the guidelines outlined in the LFS Staff and Faculty Intranet. If, after reviewing the content, you still have questions, you are welcome to email LFS Student Services for assistance.
