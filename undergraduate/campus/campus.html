Getting to Know UBC and LFS

Starting university can feel like a big change, but UBC has some great programs in place to help you find your way. Connect up with other new students and create a supportive community through any of our orientation and transition programs.

Jump Start

Jump Start is the university’s extended orientation experience: a full week in August of eating, sleeping and breathing what it's like to be a UBC student. You'll meet faculty and student leaders, learn about the tools and resources at UBC that'll help you on your academic journey, and have opportunities to experience the incredible city of Vancouver. Jump in.

Imagine UBC

Kickstart your year with Imagine UBC, a one-day, university-wide welcoming event for new students that'll see you meeting your dean and professors, connecting up with your orientation leader and finding like-minded students both inside and outside your faculty. Want to know more about what you can expect from this exciting orientation and transition experience?

LEARN MORE

Collegia

If you're not living on campus, we have a community for you! UBC Collegia are dedicated spaces where first-year commuter students can access amenities and programming to help you ease into university life. Meet other first-year students, cook a healthy meal, study a little, or catch a nap in these home-like spaces. UBC Collegia connects you with on-campus services, faculty members and peer support, making the transition into university much easier.

Gain Experience and Make Connections in LFS and at UBC​

At UBC, opportunities abound for you to level up. Get involved with research, take on a leadership position, attend career-related events and amp your career readiness through specialized programs.

LFS ACE Team

The LFS Academic and Career Engagement Team (ACE Team) is your UBC peer program that plans events in support of academic success and career development for LFS students.

To find out more about upcoming ACE Team events, follow the LFS ACE Team on Facebook, and watch out for their posters around the MacMillan and FNH Buildings.

LEARN MORE

Work Learn

Want to build career skills on campus and earn some money while you're doing it? UBC's Work Learn Program enables all current UBC students to develop their professional skills while learning in a work environment. Discover mentorship opportunities, expand your network, develop responsibility and self-awareness, and move the University toward its goals, too. Triple win.

Centre for Student Involvement & Careers

UBC provides a ton of tools, resources and programs to help you improve your own career preparation while you're here. Check the careers board, learn job search strategies, meet employers when they come to campus and more.

Clubs & Organizations

From meditation and social justice to slacklining and data science, UBC offers hundreds of different ways for you to connect with people who share your interests. There's even a Lego club! Below, we've highlighted a few of the clubs that are particularly relevant for LFS students.

Agora Café

Agora Café (conveniently located in the basement of the MacMillan Building) is an integral part of the LFS community, embracing UBC’s movement toward sustainability and food security while simultaneously providing meaningful work experiences for student volunteers. Grab a famous granola bar, or become a volunteer yourself!

LEARN MORE

LFS|US - Your Student Government

The Land and Food Systems Undergraduate Society (LFS|US) is here for you, and is committed to building a sense of community within the Faculty through organized activities. They also represent the LFS student voice on campus, ensuring our faculty is represented in University affairs.

LEARN MORE

UBC Clubs & Student Organizations

UBC has so many clubs for LFS students to get involved with. Get gardening with Roots on the Roof, drive sustainable solutions with Common Energy UBC, amp children's food education with Nutrikids, or dig deep into food processing with the Food Science Club. In fact, AMS operates more than 300 clubs for UBC students!

LEARN MORE

UBC Sprouts

Join other LFS students and get your hands dirty in this 100% volunteer and student-run group. With a focus on local, sustainably produced food, Sprouts packs food boxes, cooks up community meals, offers workshops, operates a cafe and a bulk-buying food cooperative, and most importantly, moves the needle on sustainable food production and consumption. Get in there.

LEARN MORE

UBC Cooking Club

Get cooking and give back to the community through the UBC Cooking Club. Meet other food lovers from LFS and beyond, and fast-track your cooking skills. Create two or more savoury and sweet dishes during your cooking sessions — and then dig in! The UBC Cooking Club also supports initiatives at local food banks and soup kitchens.

LEARN MORE

UBC PAW

Meet experts in veterinary medicine and explore issues in animal welfare with the UBC Pre-Veterinary and Animal Welfare Club (PAW). If you're thinking of veterinary school or are otherwise interested in working with animals, you'll find career support, opportunities for networking and a whole bunch of learning right here.
