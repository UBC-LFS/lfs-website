If the economics of food and Earth resources fascinates you, the Food and Resource Economics (FRE) program will take your engagement to a whole new level. In FRE, you'll critically analyze a wide range of economic issues in food supply chains, including the resource and environmental impacts of food production. You'll round out a solid base in economics, business, math and empirical methods with restricted and unrestricted electives, focusing your degree toward the area of food and resource economics that interests you most. Upon graduation, you'll be well set up to take your learning forward into UBC's Master of Food and Resource Economics, or into UBC's School of Public Policy and Global Affairs.

Majors Offered

Food and Resource Economics (FRE) is a major in itself.

Note: The FRE program has been approved at UBC Senate, but is awaiting formal approval from BC's Ministry of Advanced Education. Check back for updates, or get in touch with Student Services.
