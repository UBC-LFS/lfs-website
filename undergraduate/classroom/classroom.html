CLASSROOM

You may not realize it, but many of your LFS projects and assessments are preparing you for life after graduation. Things like research, presentation skills, group work and project management are examples of transferable skills that will serve you well in your future career, so keep your focus on getting the most out of each individual course.

Learning doesn’t happen only in the lecture hall, though. From global learning opportunities and co-op experiences to working on the UBC Farm and field studies that let you apply your education hands-on, there are many ways to complement your in-classroom experience and invest in your own academic success.

Enhance your classroom experience

LFS Core Series

Designed to help you make the most of your classroom experience, the LFS core series brings you together with students from other majors and the wider community to collaborate on food-system challenges. You'll be examining interdisciplinary issues and thinking through solutions that work on an economic, ecological and human level. The core series comprises two mandatory courses at the first-year level and two upper-level courses that you may take depending on your program. Learn more about the core series here, or go deep in the article here.

Land One - new for 2018!

For students in Applied Biology and Food, Nutrition and Health, Land One is a limited-enrolment first-year cohort option that LFS offers jointly with the Faculty of Forestry. This unique way of experiencing your first year of LFS lets you take your core first-year courses (math, biology, economics and communications) in an integrated format to deeply explore real-world issues from both Indigenous and Western perspectives. You'll take many of your classes with the same group of peers, as well as instructors from a variety of disciplines. Land One will set you up well for the final years of your degree. Read more about it here.

Directed Studies

Practicums and internships are designed for you to gain experience in potential fields of future employment working with animals (APBI 496) or at the UBC Farm (LFS 496). These optional courses give you hands-on opportunities to apply knowledge from your previous coursework to experiential work in wildlife rehabilitation, animal shelter management, research and farm animal management. Learn how to get going on practicums and internships here.

Undergraduate Thesis

If you're a commuter student who's not living on campus, there's a community for you! UBC Collegia are dedicated spaces where first-year commuter students can access amenities and programming to help ease you into university life. Meet other first-year students, cook a healthy meal, study a little, or catch a nap in these home-like spaces. UBC Collegia connects you with on-campus services, faculty members and peer support, making the transition into university much easier.

Improve Your Skills in the Classroom

Dreading that upcoming presentation? Curious about how to be a good group member? We don't all have the same level of skill or prior training in these things. But the UBC Learning Commons website has your back. That's where UBC experts have curated some great information and resources that'll help you master those job-ready skills. The payoff for investing some time now to learn new work strategies will prove invaluable in the future. Even if you take away only one new idea to put into practice, you're going to see improvements to your in-classroom experience and your academic success.
